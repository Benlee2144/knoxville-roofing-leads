Roof Moss & Algae Removal: What Knoxville Homeowners Need to Know

Updated February 2025 • 7 min read

Those dark streaks on your roof? That green fuzzy growth in the shady spots? You're not alone. Tennessee's humid climate makes moss and algae common problems for homeowners throughout West Knoxville, Farragut, and the surrounding areas.

The good news: it's treatable. The bad news: if ignored, it can damage your roof and shorten its lifespan.

Moss vs. Algae: What's the Difference?

Algae (Black Streaks)

Those dark streaks running down your roof are usually Gloeocapsa magma, a blue-green algae. It appears as:

  • Black or dark green streaks
  • Usually starts on north-facing slopes
  • Spreads via airborne spores
  • More of a cosmetic issue initially

Moss (Green Growth)

Moss is a plant that actually takes root on your roof. It looks like:

  • Thick, green, fuzzy patches
  • Grows in shaded, damp areas
  • Can lift shingles and trap moisture
  • More damaging than algae
Important: Moss is more serious than algae. While algae is mostly cosmetic, moss can physically damage shingles by lifting them and holding moisture against the roof surface.

Why Moss and Algae Grow on Roofs

Several factors make Tennessee roofs particularly susceptible:

  • Humidity: Our humid summers create perfect growing conditions
  • Shade: Trees that shade roofs keep them damp
  • North-facing slopes: Less sun means slower drying
  • Debris accumulation: Leaves and twigs hold moisture
  • Older shingles: Worn granules provide footholds for growth

The Damage They Cause

Algae damage:

  • Unsightly appearance (can affect home value)
  • Dark color absorbs more heat (higher cooling costs)
  • Slow degradation of shingle granules over time

Moss damage:

  • Lifts shingle edges, allowing water underneath
  • Traps moisture leading to rot
  • Roots can penetrate and damage shingles
  • Adds weight and retains water
  • Can void your roof warranty

Safe Removal Methods

For Algae

Algae is easier to treat. Here's the recommended approach:

  1. Mix 50/50 solution of water and bleach (or oxygen bleach)
  2. Apply with a garden sprayer on a cool, overcast day
  3. Let sit for 15-20 minutes
  4. Rinse gently with garden hose (NO pressure washer)
  5. Protect landscaping by wetting plants before and after

For Moss

Moss requires more effort:

  1. Brush off loose moss with a soft brush (brush downward only)
  2. Apply moss killer product and let it work
  3. Dead moss will wash away with rain over several weeks
  4. Heavy infestations may need professional removal
Never do this:
  • Use a pressure washer (destroys shingles)
  • Scrub aggressively (removes granules)
  • Walk on wet or moss-covered roof (extremely slippery)
  • Use harsh chemicals that void warranty

Prevention Strategies

Prevention is easier than treatment. Here's how to keep moss and algae from coming back:

1. Install Zinc or Copper Strips

Metal strips installed near the ridge release ions when it rains, inhibiting growth. Zinc strips are more affordable; copper is more effective.

2. Trim Trees

More sunlight means faster drying. Keep branches at least 6 feet from your roof.

3. Keep the Roof Clean

Remove leaves and debris that hold moisture. Clean gutters regularly.

4. Improve Attic Ventilation

Proper ventilation reduces moisture buildup. See our guide to roof ventilation.

5. Choose Algae-Resistant Shingles

When it's time for a new roof, choose shingles with copper granules built in. Look for "AR" (algae resistant) ratings.

Pro tip: Many newer shingles come with algae resistance built in. If your roof is over 15 years old, it probably doesn't have this protection.

DIY vs. Professional Cleaning

DIY is appropriate when:

  • You have light algae staining
  • Your roof is easily accessible (single story)
  • You're comfortable on ladders
  • The roof pitch is low

Call a professional when:

  • Moss is thick or widespread
  • You have a steep or multi-story roof
  • Growth has been there for years
  • Shingles are already damaged
  • You're not comfortable with heights

Professional soft-wash cleaning typically costs $250-$500 for an average home in Maryville, Powell, or greater Knoxville.

When Cleaning Isn't Enough

Sometimes moss and algae indicate bigger problems:

  • Extensive shingle damage: If moss has lifted or cracked shingles, cleaning won't fix the underlying damage
  • Wood rot: If moisture has penetrated, you may need repairs beyond cleaning
  • Old roof: Heavy growth on an aging roof may mean it's time for replacement

If your roof is over 20 years old and has significant moss damage, consider whether repair or replacement makes more sense.

The Bottom Line

Don't ignore moss and algae. Treat it early when it's easier and cheaper to address. Use gentle methods, prevent regrowth, and know when to call in professionals.

Your roof will thank you – with years of additional service life.
