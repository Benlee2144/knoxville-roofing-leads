Architectural Shingles: The Complete Homeowner's Guide

Updated February 2025 • 8 min read

If you're getting a new roof in Knoxville, you'll hear a lot about architectural shingles. They're the most popular roofing material in Tennessee, and for good reason. But what exactly are they, and are they right for your home?

Let's break it down.

What Are Architectural Shingles?

Architectural shingles (also called dimensional or laminated shingles) are a premium type of asphalt shingle. Unlike basic 3-tab shingles that lay flat, architectural shingles have multiple layers of material bonded together, creating a three-dimensional appearance.

This layered construction gives them:

  • A more attractive, textured look
  • Greater thickness and durability
  • Better wind resistance
  • Longer warranties

Architectural vs. 3-Tab Shingles

Feature 3-Tab Shingles Architectural Shingles
Appearance Flat, uniform Dimensional, textured
Lifespan 15-20 years 25-30+ years
Wind Rating 60-70 mph 110-130 mph
Weight ~230 lbs/square ~350 lbs/square
Cost (installed) $3.50-4.50/sq ft $4.50-6.00/sq ft
Warranty 20-25 years 30-50 years
Bottom line: Architectural shingles cost about 20-40% more upfront but last significantly longer and look much better. For most Knoxville homeowners, they're the better investment.

Benefits of Architectural Shingles

1. Superior Durability

The multi-layer construction makes architectural shingles much tougher than 3-tab. They handle Tennessee weather better – the summer heat, winter ice, and everything in between.

2. Better Wind Resistance

Most architectural shingles are rated for winds of 110-130 mph. That matters when summer storms roll through Powell, Halls, and the rest of Knox County.

3. Improved Curb Appeal

The dimensional look mimics more expensive materials like wood shakes or slate. They add visual interest that flat shingles simply can't match.

4. Longer Warranties

Manufacturers stand behind architectural shingles with warranties of 30-50 years, compared to 20-25 years for 3-tab. Some premium lines even offer lifetime warranties.

5. Better Resale Value

A roof with architectural shingles is a selling point. Homebuyers recognize the quality and the remaining lifespan, which can help justify your asking price.

Cost of Architectural Shingles in Knoxville

For a typical 2,000 square foot home in the Knoxville area, expect to pay:

  • Materials only: $3,000 - $5,000
  • Installation: $4,000 - $7,000
  • Total project: $7,000 - $12,000

Prices vary based on roof complexity, removal of old materials, and the specific shingle chosen. Premium designer shingles can push costs higher.

Best Colors for Tennessee Homes

Color choice affects both aesthetics and energy efficiency. In our climate:

Popular choices:

  • Weathered Wood – versatile brown/gray blend
  • Charcoal – classic, hides dirt well
  • Barkwood – warm brown tones
  • Slate – sophisticated gray

Energy considerations:

  • Lighter colors reflect more heat (lower cooling costs)
  • Darker colors absorb heat (can shorten shingle life slightly)
  • Look for "cool roof" rated shingles if energy efficiency is a priority

Many homeowners in Farragut and Bearden choose earth tones that complement Tennessee's natural landscape.

Installation Considerations

Proper installation is critical. Things your contractor should do:

  • Use manufacturer-specified underlayment
  • Follow nailing patterns exactly
  • Install proper ventilation
  • Use starter strips and hip/ridge caps
  • Ensure flashing is properly integrated

Improper installation can void the warranty, so choose your contractor carefully. Read our guide on questions to ask before hiring a roofer.

Maintenance Tips

Architectural shingles are low-maintenance, but some care extends their life:

  1. Keep gutters clean
  2. Trim overhanging branches
  3. Remove debris that accumulates
  4. Address moss or algae quickly
  5. Schedule inspections every few years

Are Architectural Shingles Right for You?

Architectural shingles are the best choice for most Knoxville homeowners. They offer the right balance of cost, durability, and appearance for our climate.

Consider other options if:

  • You need the absolute lowest cost (3-tab)
  • You want maximum lifespan (metal roofing)
  • You're matching a historic home's original materials
