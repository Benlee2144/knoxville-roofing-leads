Summer Roof Maintenance Tips for Knoxville Homeowners

Updated February 2025 • 6 min read

Tennessee summers are brutal on roofs. Between the intense UV rays, afternoon thunderstorms, and temperatures that regularly hit the 90s, your roof takes a beating from June through September. A little preventive maintenance now can save you thousands in repairs later.

Here's what every Farragut, West Knoxville, and greater Knox County homeowner should do to protect their roof this summer.

1. Schedule a Professional Inspection

Summer is ideal for roof inspections. The weather is predictable, and any issues found can be addressed before fall storms arrive.

Pro tip: The best time to schedule is early summer (June) or late summer (August-September). Mid-summer heat makes roof work uncomfortable and can affect material installation.

A professional will check:

  • Shingle condition and granule loss
  • Flashing around vents, chimneys, and skylights
  • Signs of heat damage or warping
  • Gutter attachment and drainage
  • Attic ventilation effectiveness

2. Clean Your Gutters

Summer storms dump a lot of water fast. Clogged gutters can't handle the volume, leading to water backing up under your shingles or overflowing against your foundation.

What to do:

  • Remove all debris from gutters and downspouts
  • Check for proper slope (water should flow toward downspouts)
  • Ensure downspouts direct water at least 4 feet from your foundation
  • Consider gutter guards if you have overhanging trees

3. Trim Overhanging Branches

Trees provide great shade, but branches that hang over your roof create problems:

  • They drop debris that clogs gutters
  • They scrape against shingles in the wind
  • They provide a highway for squirrels and other critters
  • They create shaded, damp areas where moss and algae grow

Keep branches trimmed at least 6 feet back from your roof. For large trees, hire a professional arborist.

4. Check Attic Ventilation

Warning: Poor attic ventilation in summer can push temperatures above 150°F. This bakes your shingles from below, shortening their lifespan significantly.

Proper ventilation requires both intake vents (usually in the soffits) and exhaust vents (ridge vents or roof vents). Check that:

  • Soffit vents aren't blocked by insulation
  • Ridge vents are clear of debris
  • Bathroom and dryer vents exhaust outside, not into the attic
  • Attic fans (if you have them) are working properly

5. Look for Storm Damage

Knoxville gets its share of summer thunderstorms, sometimes with hail. After any significant storm, do a visual check:

  • Look for missing or lifted shingles
  • Check for debris impact marks
  • Examine gutters and downspouts for dents
  • Look for granules in your gutters (a sign of hail damage)

If you spot damage, document everything for your insurance claim before calling for repairs.

6. Address Minor Issues Immediately

Small problems become big problems fast in summer heat. A tiny crack in caulking or a slightly lifted shingle can let water in during the next storm.

Don't wait on:

  • Cracked or missing caulk around flashing
  • Loose or missing shingles
  • Small rust spots on metal components
  • Damaged boot seals around pipe vents

7. Consider Reflective Coatings

If your roof is dark-colored and you're dealing with high cooling bills, a reflective roof coating might help. These coatings can:

  • Reduce roof surface temperature by 50-60°F
  • Lower attic temperatures significantly
  • Decrease air conditioning costs
  • Extend shingle life by reducing thermal stress

This is especially worth considering for homes in Maryville, Oak Ridge, and other areas with limited tree cover.

Summer Roof Maintenance Checklist

Here's your quick reference checklist:

  1. Schedule professional inspection (early or late summer)
  2. Clean gutters and check drainage
  3. Trim overhanging branches
  4. Verify attic ventilation is working
  5. Check for storm damage after severe weather
  6. Address minor repairs promptly
  7. Consider reflective coating for dark roofs

When to Call a Professional

Some maintenance tasks are fine for homeowners. Others require professional help:

DIY-friendly:

  • Visual inspections from the ground
  • Gutter cleaning (single-story homes)
  • Checking attic vents from inside

Call a pro:

  • Walking on the roof
  • Any actual repairs
  • Tree trimming near power lines
  • Multi-story gutter cleaning
