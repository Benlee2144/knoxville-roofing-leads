Energy-Efficient Roofing: Lower Your Bills and Stay Comfortable

Updated February 2025 • 7 min read

Your roof does more than keep rain out. It plays a major role in your home's energy efficiency. The right roofing choices can significantly reduce your heating and cooling costs – something that matters a lot during Knoxville's hot summers and cold winters.

Here's what homeowners in West Knoxville, Farragut, and throughout Knox County need to know about energy-efficient roofing.

How Your Roof Affects Energy Use

Your roof impacts energy efficiency in several ways:

  • Solar heat absorption: Dark roofs can reach 150°F+ on summer days
  • Heat transfer: Absorbed heat moves into your attic and living space
  • Ventilation: Proper airflow affects attic temperatures
  • Insulation interaction: Your roof works with insulation to control temperatures
Potential savings: Energy-efficient roofing can reduce cooling costs by 10-25% according to the Department of Energy. For a typical Knoxville home, that's $150-$400 per year.

Cool Roof Technology

"Cool roofs" are designed to reflect more sunlight and absorb less heat. They achieve this through:

1. Reflective Surfaces

Materials that reflect solar radiation instead of absorbing it. Measured by "solar reflectance" – higher is better.

2. Thermal Emittance

The ability to release absorbed heat quickly. High thermal emittance means the roof cools down faster.

3. Solar Reflectance Index (SRI)

A combined measure of both properties. ENERGY STAR requires SRI of at least 25 for steep-slope roofs.

Energy-Efficient Roofing Options

Cool Asphalt Shingles

Modern "cool" shingles have reflective granules that reflect more solar energy than traditional shingles.

  • Cost: Similar to standard architectural shingles
  • Savings: 7-15% reduction in cooling costs
  • Best for: Most residential applications
  • Brands: GAF Timberline Cool Series, Owens Corning Duration COOL

Metal Roofing

Metal roofs naturally reflect solar radiation and release heat quickly. With reflective coatings, they're even more efficient.

  • Cost: 2-3x more than asphalt
  • Savings: 10-25% reduction in cooling costs
  • Best for: Long-term ownership, maximum efficiency
  • Lifespan: 40-70 years

Learn more in our guide to metal roofing.

Tile Roofing

Clay and concrete tiles have natural thermal mass and airflow underneath that improves efficiency.

  • Cost: 2-4x more than asphalt
  • Savings: 15-20% reduction in cooling costs
  • Best for: Mediterranean or Spanish-style homes
  • Lifespan: 50-100 years

Cool Roof Coatings

Reflective coatings can be applied to existing roofs, particularly flat or low-slope roofs.

  • Cost: $0.75-$1.50 per square foot
  • Savings: 10-15% reduction in cooling costs
  • Best for: Flat roofs, extending roof life
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years before recoating

The Role of Color

Color matters, but technology matters more. A cool roof coating on a dark surface can outperform a standard light-colored roof.

That said, all else being equal:

  • Light colors reflect 55-90% of sunlight
  • Dark colors reflect only 5-25%

If you want a darker aesthetic, look for cool-rated shingles in darker colors. They use special pigments that reflect infrared light while maintaining the dark appearance.

Tennessee consideration: Our climate has both hot summers and cold winters. A highly reflective roof saves on cooling but provides less passive solar heating in winter. For most homes, the summer savings outweigh the minor winter impact.

Beyond the Roof Surface

For maximum energy efficiency, consider the complete system:

Attic Insulation

Even the best roof won't help if heat transfers through inadequate insulation. We recommend R-38 to R-60 for Knox County homes. See our attic insulation guide.

Radiant Barriers

Reflective foil installed in the attic blocks radiant heat transfer. Can reduce cooling costs by 5-10%.

Proper Ventilation

Good ventilation removes hot air from the attic before it can transfer into your living space. Read our ventilation guide.

Air Sealing

Gaps and cracks let conditioned air escape. Sealing these improves efficiency regardless of roof type.

Rebates and Incentives

Several programs can help offset the cost of energy-efficient roofing:

TVA EnergyRight

TVA offers rebates and financing for qualifying energy improvements. Check with Lenoir City Utilities Board or your local power company for current programs.

Federal Tax Credits

The Inflation Reduction Act provides tax credits for certain energy-efficient home improvements. Metal and asphalt roofing that meets ENERGY STAR requirements may qualify.

Manufacturer Rebates

Some manufacturers offer rebates for cool roof products. Ask your contractor about current promotions.

Is an Energy-Efficient Roof Worth It?

Let's do the math for a typical Knoxville home:

Scenario: Replacing a standard roof with cool-rated shingles

  • Additional cost: $500-$1,000
  • Annual savings: $200-$400
  • Payback period: 2-5 years

When you're already replacing your roof, the incremental cost of energy-efficient materials is minimal – and it pays for itself quickly.

Questions to Ask Your Contractor

  1. Do you offer ENERGY STAR-rated roofing products?
  2. What's the SRI (Solar Reflectance Index) of this material?
  3. How does the warranty compare to standard materials?
  4. Are there any current rebates or incentives available?
  5. Will this affect the appearance of my home?

The Bottom Line

Energy-efficient roofing makes sense for most Knoxville homeowners, especially when replacing an existing roof. The additional cost is modest, the savings are real, and you'll enjoy a more comfortable home in every season.
