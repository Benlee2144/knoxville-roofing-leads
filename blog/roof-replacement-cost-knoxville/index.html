Roof Replacement Cost in Knoxville, TN: 2024 Complete Price Guide

Updated February 2024 | 8 min read

Wondering how much a new roof costs in Knoxville? Whether you're dealing with storm damage, an aging roof, or planning ahead, understanding local pricing helps you budget accurately and avoid overpaying. In this guide, we break down exactly what Knoxville homeowners pay for roof replacements in 2024.

Quick Answer: Knoxville Roof Replacement Costs

  • Average cost: $8,500 - $15,000 (most common)
  • Low end: $5,500 - $8,000 (small roof, basic shingles)
  • High end: $20,000 - $35,000+ (large roof, premium materials)
  • Per square foot: $3.50 - $7.50

Factors That Affect Your Roof Cost in Knoxville

1. Roof Size & Complexity

Roofers measure in "squares" (100 sq ft each). The average Knoxville home has a 20-25 square roof. However, complexity matters too:

  • Simple gable roof: Straightforward, lower labor cost
  • Hip roof: More cuts, 10-15% more labor
  • Multiple valleys, dormers: 15-25% premium
  • Steep pitch (8/12 or higher): Safety equipment adds 10-20%

2. Tear-Off & Disposal

Most Knoxville roofing projects require removing the old roof first. Expect to pay:

  • Single layer removal: $1.00 - $1.50 per sq ft
  • Two layers: $1.50 - $2.50 per sq ft
  • Dumpster & disposal fees: $300 - $600

3. Decking & Structural Repairs

Once the old shingles are off, rotten or damaged decking must be replaced. Tennessee humidity and storm damage make this common:

  • Plywood decking: $70 - $100 per 4x8 sheet installed
  • Rafter/truss repair: $200 - $500+ per section

Tip: A good contractor will estimate likely repairs but give you a final price after inspecting the decking.

4. Permits & Code Requirements

Knox County requires permits for roof replacements. Most contractors include this in their quote:

  • Permit cost: $75 - $200
  • Code upgrades (if needed): Ice & water shield, additional ventilation

5. Time of Year

Roofing in Knoxville is busiest from spring through fall. You may find better pricing in:

  • Late fall/early winter: Lower demand = potential discounts
  • January-February: Slowest season (weather permitting)

Average Costs by Knoxville Neighborhood

Prices can vary by area due to home sizes, roof styles, and local competition:

  • West Knoxville/Farragut: $10,000 - $18,000 (larger homes, architectural shingles standard)
  • Powell/Halls: $8,000 - $14,000 (mix of ranch and two-story homes)
  • Downtown/Old North: $9,000 - $16,000 (older homes, sometimes complex)
  • Maryville/Blount County: $8,000 - $15,000 (similar to Knoxville pricing)
  • Oak Ridge: $7,500 - $14,000 (many mid-century homes)

How to Save Money on Your Knoxville Roof Replacement

1. Get Multiple Quotes (Most Important!)

This is the single best way to save. Roofing quotes in Knoxville can vary by $3,000 - $7,000+ for the exact same job. We recommend getting at least 3 quotes from licensed contractors.

2. Choose Architectural Shingles Over Premium

For most Knoxville homes, architectural shingles offer the best value. They last 25-30 years and look great without the premium price tag.

3. Consider Timing

If your roof isn't an emergency, scheduling in late fall or winter can sometimes save 5-15%.

4. Check for Insurance Coverage

If your roof has storm or hail damage, your homeowners insurance may cover replacement minus your deductible. Many Knoxville contractors offer free inspections and help with the claims process.

5. Don't Skip the Important Stuff

Proper ventilation, quality underlayment, and ice & water shield aren't places to cut corners. They protect your investment for decades.

Red Flags to Watch For

Protect yourself from unscrupulous contractors:

  • Unusually low quotes: If it's $4,000 less than everyone else, ask why
  • No written contract: Everything should be in writing
  • Large deposits: Never pay more than 10-30% upfront
  • No license or insurance: Verify with the Tennessee Board of Licensing
  • Pressure tactics: Legitimate contractors don't push "today only" deals

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a roof replacement take in Knoxville?

Most residential roof replacements take 1-3 days. Larger or complex roofs may take 4-5 days. Weather delays are common in East Tennessee, especially in spring.

Do I need to be home during the roof replacement?

No, you don't need to be home. However, you should secure pets, move vehicles away from the work area, and ensure the contractor can access water and electricity if needed.

Will my homeowners insurance cover a new roof?

Insurance typically covers roof damage from storms, hail, fire, or fallen trees. It usually doesn't cover normal wear and tear. Many Knoxville contractors offer free inspections to help determine if your damage is covered.

How often should I replace my roof in Tennessee?

It depends on the material: 3-tab shingles last 15-20 years, architectural shingles 25-30 years, and metal roofs 40-70 years. Tennessee's heat, humidity, and storms can shorten these lifespans.
