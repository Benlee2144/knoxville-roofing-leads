Roof Noise Solutions: Quieting Your Knoxville Home

Updated February 2025 • 6 min read

A noisy roof can disrupt sleep, make it hard to concentrate, and generally drive you crazy. Whether it's rain that sounds like a drum solo, mysterious popping and cracking, or critters running overhead, there are solutions.

Here's how to identify and address common roof noise problems in Knoxville homes.

Types of Roof Noise and Their Causes

Rain Noise

What it sounds like: Loud drumming or tapping during rainfall

Most common on: Metal roofs, skylights, flat roofs

Cause: Direct impact of raindrops on roof surface without adequate sound dampening

Thermal Expansion (Popping/Cracking)

What it sounds like: Popping, cracking, or snapping sounds, especially at sunrise/sunset

Most common on: Metal roofs, but occurs on all roof types

Cause: Materials expanding as they heat up and contracting as they cool

Wind Noise

What it sounds like: Whistling, howling, or flapping

Most common on: Any roof with loose components or gaps

Cause: Wind moving over/under loose materials, through gaps, or around vents

Animal Activity

What it sounds like: Scratching, scurrying, or thumping

Most common on: Any roof with nearby trees or entry points

Cause: Squirrels, raccoons, birds, or other animals walking on or entering the roof/attic

Solutions for Rain Noise

For Metal Roofs

Metal roofs can be quieter than shingles when properly installed, but improper installation causes noise issues.

Solutions:
  • Add solid sheathing: Metal installed over purlins (strips) is louder than metal over solid plywood/OSB decking
  • Install underlayment: Sound-deadening underlayment between decking and metal significantly reduces noise
  • Add attic insulation: More insulation means more sound absorption
  • Fasten properly: Loose panels vibrate more and make more noise

If you're considering a new metal roof, see our metal roofing pros and cons guide for more on noise considerations.

For Skylights

Skylights can amplify rain noise dramatically.

Solutions:
  • Install skylight covers: External covers dampen sound
  • Use interior shades: Cellular shades provide some sound reduction
  • Choose better skylights: When replacing, choose laminated glass or acrylic domes designed for quiet
  • Add curb insulation: Properly insulated curbs reduce sound transmission

Solutions for Thermal Expansion Noise

Some thermal movement is unavoidable, but excessive noise can be addressed:

Solutions:
  • Check fastener patterns: Improper fastening can cause more movement and noise
  • Allow for expansion: Metal roofs need room to move; overly tight installation increases noise
  • Add insulation: Reduces temperature extremes the roof experiences
  • Choose appropriate colors: Lighter colors reduce heat absorption and temperature swings

If you recently got a new roof and it's making expansion noises, give it time. Some settling noise in the first few months is normal.

Solutions for Wind Noise

Wind noise usually indicates something that needs fixing:

Solutions:
  • Secure loose shingles or flashing: Flapping materials create noise
  • Seal gaps: Fill gaps where wind can whistle through
  • Check vent caps: Loose or damaged vent caps can howl in wind
  • Inspect ridge vents: Improperly installed ridge vents can create wind noise
  • Trim trees: Branches scraping on roof during wind are noisy and damaging

Wind noise that starts suddenly often indicates storm damage that should be inspected.

Solutions for Animal Noise

If you hear scurrying or scratching, you likely have visitors:

Solutions:
  • Trim trees: Keep branches at least 6 feet from the roof to prevent easy access
  • Seal entry points: Check soffits, vents, and gaps for entry points and seal them
  • Install vent guards: Wire mesh over vents prevents animal entry
  • Call professionals: If animals are already inside, use wildlife removal services before sealing

Common culprits in West Knoxville, Farragut, and suburban areas include squirrels, raccoons, and occasionally birds.

Attic Insulation: The Universal Solution

Additional attic insulation helps with almost all roof noise:

  • Absorbs sound from rain and hail
  • Reduces thermal cycling (less expansion noise)
  • Muffles animal activity sounds
  • Blocks wind noise
  • Also improves energy efficiency

Many Knoxville homes have less insulation than recommended. Current guidelines suggest R-38 to R-60 for our area. See our attic insulation guide for details.

When to Call a Professional

Some noise issues require professional assessment:

  • New or sudden noises (may indicate damage)
  • Noise accompanied by leaks or water stains
  • Sounds indicating structural movement
  • Animals that have entered the attic
  • Noises you can't identify

A roofing professional can identify the source and recommend appropriate solutions.

Noise Considerations for New Roofs

If you're getting a new roof and noise is a concern:

Material Selection

  • Asphalt shingles are generally quietest
  • Metal can be quiet with proper installation
  • Tile can amplify sound if not properly installed

Installation Details

  • Solid decking under metal is much quieter than spaced purlins
  • Sound-deadening underlayment helps significantly
  • Proper fastening reduces thermal expansion noise
  • Quality materials and installation matter

Discuss noise concerns with your contractor before the project begins. Additional sound-reducing measures are much easier to add during installation than after.

The Bottom Line

Roof noise has solutions. Whether it's rain, thermal expansion, wind, or critters, you don't have to live with the racket. Identify the source, apply appropriate solutions, and enjoy a quieter home.
