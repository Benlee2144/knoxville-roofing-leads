How to Choose the Right Roofing Contractor in Knoxville

Updated February 2025 • 9 min read

Choosing a roofing contractor is one of the most important home improvement decisions you'll make. A great contractor delivers quality work that protects your home for decades. A bad one can leave you with leaks, damage, and regret.

Here's how homeowners in West Knoxville, Farragut, Powell, and throughout Knox County can find the right roofer for their project.

Step 1: Verify Basic Credentials

Before anything else, confirm these essentials:

Tennessee Contractor's License

Tennessee requires contractors to be licensed for projects over $25,000. Verify at verify.tn.gov. The license should be active and in good standing.

Insurance Coverage

Require proof of:

  • General liability: At least $1 million (protects you if they damage your property)
  • Workers' compensation: Covers injuries to workers on your property
Important: Don't just accept a certificate – call the insurance company to verify coverage is current. Policies can be cancelled after a certificate is issued.

Physical Business Location

A real business address (not a PO box) indicates stability. You should be able to find them if issues arise later.

Step 2: Research Their Reputation

Online Reviews

Check multiple sources:

  • Google Business reviews
  • Facebook recommendations
  • Better Business Bureau (BBB)
  • Angi (formerly Angie's List)
  • HomeAdvisor

Look for patterns in reviews. A few complaints among many positives is normal; consistent complaints about the same issues are red flags.

Years in Business

Longevity matters. Companies that have served Knoxville for 10+ years have built a reputation worth protecting. New companies aren't necessarily bad, but they're unproven.

References

Ask for 5-10 recent references and actually call a few. Questions to ask:

  • Was the work completed on time and on budget?
  • How was communication throughout the project?
  • Did they clean up properly when done?
  • Have you had any issues since completion?
  • Would you hire them again?

Step 3: Get Multiple Quotes

Always get at least three written quotes. This helps you:

  • Understand fair market pricing
  • Compare what's included
  • Identify outliers (too high or suspiciously low)
  • Evaluate how contractors communicate

What a Quote Should Include

  • Detailed scope of work
  • Materials to be used (brand, type, color)
  • Removal and disposal of old materials
  • Underlayment and flashing specifications
  • Ventilation work
  • Timeline for completion
  • Payment schedule
  • Warranty information
Comparing quotes: Don't just compare total price. Compare what you're getting. A lower bid might mean cheaper materials, skipped steps, or corners cut.

Step 4: Meet and Evaluate

Have contractors visit your property. This gives you valuable information:

How Thoroughly Do They Inspect?

A good contractor doesn't quote from the ground or a quick glance. They should:

  • Examine the roof up close (or use drone/binoculars for steep roofs)
  • Check the attic if accessible
  • Look at ventilation
  • Inspect flashing, valleys, and penetrations
  • Note the number of layers currently installed

How Well Do They Communicate?

  • Do they explain things clearly?
  • Do they answer questions patiently?
  • Do they pressure you or give you space to decide?
  • Are they responsive to calls and messages?

Do They Seem Professional?

  • Are they on time for appointments?
  • Do they have professional vehicles and equipment?
  • Are their workers uniformed or identifiable?

Step 5: Understand the Contract

Never start work without a written contract. It should include:

Essential Contract Elements

  1. Full company name, address, license number
  2. Complete description of work to be performed
  3. All materials to be used (specific brands and products)
  4. Total price and payment schedule
  5. Project start date and estimated completion
  6. Who obtains permits
  7. Warranty details (workmanship and materials)
  8. Change order process
  9. Cleanup and debris removal
  10. Insurance requirements

Payment Terms to Expect

  • Deposit: 10-30% is typical (never more than 1/3)
  • Progress payment: After materials are delivered and/or work begins
  • Final payment: Upon satisfactory completion

Red Flags to Avoid

Walk away if a contractor:

  • Can't provide license or insurance documentation
  • Pressures you to sign immediately
  • Offers a price "only good today"
  • Asks for large upfront payment (50%+)
  • Wants to skip permits
  • Has no physical business address
  • Offers to waive your insurance deductible
  • Shows up uninvited after a storm
  • Can't provide references
  • Has unusually low prices compared to competitors

Manufacturer Certifications

Many top contractors are certified by roofing manufacturers:

  • GAF Master Elite: Top 3% of contractors nationwide
  • Owens Corning Preferred: Vetted and trained by manufacturer
  • CertainTeed SELECT ShingleMaster: Rigorous certification program

These certifications typically require:

  • Proof of proper licensing and insurance
  • Good reputation and business practices
  • Training on proper installation methods
  • Ongoing education requirements

Certified contractors can often offer enhanced warranties that non-certified contractors cannot.

Questions to Ask

Use our comprehensive list in 12 Questions to Ask Before Hiring a Roofer. Key questions include:

  • How long have you been in business in Knoxville?
  • Will you pull the permits?
  • Who will be on site managing the project?
  • What happens if we find unexpected damage?
  • What warranty do you offer on workmanship?
  • How do you handle debris and cleanup?

Local Considerations

Contractors serving Maryville, Oak Ridge, Sevierville, and the broader Knoxville area should understand local factors:

  • Tennessee licensing requirements
  • Knox County permit processes
  • Local weather patterns and material recommendations
  • HOA requirements in various neighborhoods
  • Historic district rules where applicable

The Bottom Line

Choosing a roofing contractor takes time and effort, but it's worth it. A quality contractor delivers work that protects your home for decades. Take the time to vet thoroughly, compare carefully, and trust your instincts. When something feels wrong, it usually is.
