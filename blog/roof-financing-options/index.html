Roof Financing Options: How to Afford Your New Roof

Updated February 2025 • 8 min read

A new roof in Knoxville typically costs $8,000 to $15,000 – not exactly spare change. If your roof needs replacement but your savings account isn't ready, you have options. Here's how homeowners in Farragut, West Knoxville, and throughout Knox County are financing their roofing projects.

1. Contractor Financing

What it is

Many roofing companies partner with financing companies to offer payment plans directly. You apply through the contractor, and if approved, the work can often begin immediately.

Pros

  • Convenient one-stop process
  • Often promotional rates (0% for 12-18 months)
  • Quick approval process
  • No home equity required

Cons

  • Higher rates after promo period
  • May limit contractor choices
  • Deferred interest can be costly
  • Requires good credit

Watch out for: Deferred interest promotions. If you don't pay off the balance before the promotional period ends, you may owe interest on the entire original amount retroactively.

2. Home Equity Loan

What it is

A home equity loan lets you borrow a lump sum against the equity in your home. You get a fixed interest rate and fixed monthly payments.

Pros

  • Lower interest rates than personal loans
  • Interest may be tax-deductible
  • Fixed, predictable payments
  • Larger amounts available

Cons

  • Your home is collateral
  • Closing costs apply
  • Takes longer to process
  • Requires sufficient equity

Home equity loans are a good choice if you have significant equity and want the lowest possible interest rate. Current rates are typically 7-9% for well-qualified borrowers.

3. Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC)

What it is

A HELOC works like a credit card secured by your home. You're approved for a credit line and can draw from it as needed.

Pros

  • Flexibility to use what you need
  • Only pay interest on what you use
  • Can be reused for future projects
  • Lower rates than credit cards

Cons

  • Variable interest rates
  • Your home is collateral
  • Temptation to overspend
  • Annual fees may apply

HELOCs are ideal if you have multiple home improvements planned or want flexibility. Just be disciplined about paying it down.

4. Personal Loan

What it is

An unsecured loan from a bank, credit union, or online lender. No collateral required – approval is based on your creditworthiness.

Pros

  • No home equity needed
  • Fast funding (sometimes same day)
  • Fixed rates and payments
  • Home isn't at risk

Cons

  • Higher interest rates
  • Shorter repayment terms
  • Requires good credit for best rates
  • May have origination fees

Personal loans work well for smaller roofing projects or when you don't want to use your home as collateral. Rates typically range from 8-15% for good credit.

5. Credit Cards

What it is

Using existing credit cards or opening a new card with a 0% introductory APR offer.

Pros

  • Immediate availability
  • 0% intro APR offers available
  • Rewards points possible
  • No application process for existing cards

Cons

  • Very high rates after intro period
  • May not have enough credit limit
  • Can hurt credit utilization ratio
  • Easy to carry balance too long

Credit cards make sense only if you can pay off the balance during a 0% promotional period. Otherwise, rates of 20%+ make this an expensive option.

6. Insurance Claim

If your roof damage is from a covered event (storm, hail, fallen tree), your homeowner's insurance may pay for replacement minus your deductible.

Steps to take:

  1. Document the damage thoroughly
  2. Contact your insurance company
  3. Get a professional inspection
  4. Meet with the insurance adjuster
  5. Get repair estimates

Which Option Is Best for You?

Best overall value: Home equity loan (if you have equity and can wait for processing)

Best for speed: Contractor financing or personal loan

Best for flexibility: HELOC

Best for avoiding debt: Insurance claim (if applicable) or saving up

Questions to Ask Before Financing

  1. What is the APR (not just the interest rate)?
  2. Are there origination fees or closing costs?
  3. What happens if I pay off early (prepayment penalties)?
  4. Is the rate fixed or variable?
  5. What are the monthly payments?
  6. What's the total cost over the life of the loan?

Can't Afford a Full Replacement?

If financing a full replacement isn't feasible right now, consider:

  • Repairs instead of replacement: When repair makes more sense
  • Phased approach: Address critical issues first
  • Different materials: 3-tab shingles cost less than architectural
  • Off-season scheduling: Some contractors offer winter discounts

Just don't delay necessary repairs. A $500 repair today can prevent a $10,000 problem tomorrow.

Local Resources

Homeowners in Oak Ridge, Maryville, Clinton, and other Knox County communities may qualify for:

  • TVA energy loans: For improvements that increase energy efficiency
  • USDA Rural Development loans: For qualifying rural areas
  • Local credit unions: Often have better rates than banks
  • Weatherization assistance: For income-qualifying households

The Bottom Line

Don't let cost prevent you from addressing roof problems. Between contractor financing, home equity options, and personal loans, there's likely a solution that fits your situation. Just take time to compare options and understand the true cost before signing anything.
