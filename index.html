Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a new roof cost in Knoxville, TN?

The average cost of a new roof in Knoxville ranges from $8,000 to $25,000 depending on the size of your home, materials chosen, and complexity of the job. Asphalt shingles are the most affordable option, while metal and tile roofs cost more but last longer. Getting multiple quotes can save you up to 40% on your roofing project.

Is this roofing quote service really free?

Yes, our service is 100% free for homeowners. We connect you with up to 3 licensed, insured roofing contractors in Knoxville who will provide free estimates. There's absolutely no obligation to hire anyone. Contractors pay us a small fee for the connection, so you pay nothing.

How quickly will I receive quotes?

Most homeowners receive quotes within 24-48 hours of submitting their request. For emergency repairs like active leaks or storm damage, contractors often respond the same day. The more details you provide about your project, the faster and more accurate your quotes will be.

How do I know if I need a roof repair or full replacement?

Signs you may need a repair: a few missing shingles, minor leaks, or localized damage. Signs you may need a replacement: roof is 20+ years old, widespread damage, sagging, multiple leaks, or visible daylight through the roof. Our contractors provide free inspections to help you decide.

Will my homeowners insurance cover roof damage?

Most homeowners insurance policies cover roof damage caused by sudden events like storms, hail, fallen trees, or fire. However, damage from normal wear and tear or lack of maintenance typically isn't covered. Many Knoxville contractors in our network specialize in insurance claims and can help maximize your coverage.

What roofing materials are best for Tennessee weather?

Asphalt shingles are the most popular choice in Knoxville due to their affordability and durability against our hot summers and occasional storms. Metal roofing is gaining popularity for its 50+ year lifespan and energy efficiency. Architectural shingles offer a balance of aesthetics and performance.
